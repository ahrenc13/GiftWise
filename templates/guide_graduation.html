How We Picked These

Graduation gifts fail for one of two reasons: they're too generic (another frame, another candle) or they're too specific and miss who the person actually is. We aimed for the middle — gifts with a wide enough strike zone to work for most grads, but specific enough to feel considered.

They'll use it for at least a year. Nothing one-time. Everything here has a shelf life measured in years, not weeks. A laptop, headphones, luggage, a year of game access — daily-use gifts beat anything decorative.

They wouldn't buy it themselves. The best gifts are things the recipient genuinely wants but wouldn't justify spending on themselves. A $550 open-box laptop falls into this category. So does a craft beer subscription at $43/month.

Common Questions

What is a good graduation gift that isn't money?

Tech upgrades are the most universally used graduation gift — an open-box refurbished laptop from Tech For Less ($450–$550) beats any new laptop in its price range with full warranty. Subscriptions they'll use for a year (Xbox Game Pass, a meal kit, streaming) feel like cash but more thoughtful. For someone entering the workforce, quality travel gear like an Away carry-on is a practical luxury they wouldn't buy themselves.

What do high school graduates actually want?

Cash-equivalent practical things work best — but done thoughtfully. An Amazon gift card works but feels lazy. Better: a subscription they'll actually use for the next year (Xbox Game Pass, a meal kit, Netflix), or a tech upgrade like noise-cancelling headphones. These feel like a real gift, cost similar to cash, and get used daily for months.

What are good college graduation gifts?

For someone entering the workforce, professional gear lands well: quality luggage, a nice watch, or a Kindle for commuting. For someone who's been living on ramen, an Instant Pot or a Goldbelly gift card is genuinely useful. For the adventurous grad, an America the Beautiful National Parks Pass ($80) covers 2,000+ federal sites for a year — the gift of being able to go.
