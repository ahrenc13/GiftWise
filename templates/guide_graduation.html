Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good graduation gift that isn't money?

Tech upgrades are universally loved — a refurbished MacBook or a pair of Bose headphones will outlast any frame. Subscriptions are another strong option: a year of Xbox Game Pass, Netflix, or a meal kit service gives them something useful for the next chapter. Practical gear for wherever they're headed (a quality carry-on, an Instant Pot if they're moving out) lands well because it solves a real problem they're about to have.

What do high school graduates actually want?

Cash-equivalent practical things. An Amazon gift card works but feels lazy; better is a subscription they'll use for the next year — Xbox Game Pass, a streaming service, or a meal kit. Tech upgrades are universally loved at any age. If you want something they'll remember, a quality pair of headphones or a refurbished laptop is the kind of gift that follows them through the next four years.

What are good college graduation gifts?

Professional gear for their career path lands especially well — a nice watch, a quality portfolio bag or briefcase, or a Kindle for the reading-heavy professions (law, medicine, finance). An experience is also strong at this stage: a weekend trip, a cooking class, or a celebration dinner. For someone entering the workforce, a quality work bag or carry-on signals that you take their next chapter seriously.
